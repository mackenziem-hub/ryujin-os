How the memory system is built

Three stores of memory, one live layer on top.

The memory is not one thing. It is three stores with different jobs, plus a live layer that reads the business fresh every load. The stores were healthy. The live layer is where it broke.

STORE 1 · SOURCE OF TRUTH
The Supabase spine

session_entries + context_principles. The canonical cross-machine brain. Every save writes a row here; every load rebuilds from it. Shared by all machines and Cat.

STORE 2 · DERIVED
SESSION_CONTEXT.md

Rebuilt from the spine at every load. Human-readable working state. Never hand-edited, it is a view of the rows.

STORE 3 · DURABLE RULES
Auto-memory + MEMORY.md

The timeless principles (pricing SOP, Jewels rules, this feedback). Loaded into context every session.

The live layer (this is what broke)

On top of the stores sits the load-scan and the cron agents: the daily brief, the snapshot, the inbox triage. They read the business live. They had no freshness contract and no observability, so when one degraded, nothing said so.

04 / 09
Root cause 1 of 3

The brief failed silently, then dressed it up as calm.

The daily brief is the system's one synthesis of "what matters today." Three quiet failures hollowed it out, and each one was hidden, not flagged.

Dead Google Calendar auth, swallowed

The calendar fetch threw, a catch turned it into null, and the brief printed "Empty calendar" as if the day were genuinely clear. The failure was disguised as a fact.

Thin agent findings, no money

The pipeline agents returned little, so the brief never named the $48K of warm deals going cold. The most important numbers in the business were absent.

Raw task titles as priorities

With nothing real to say, the top 3 collapsed to "Quote required" twice. A templated shrug standing in for a plan.

05 / 09
Root cause 2 of 3

Nothing watched the watchers.

The agents that produce your daily intelligence could not be seen. They never wrote a run record, so silent degradation was undetectable by design.

The blind spot

briefing, daily, cashflow, watchdog, heartbeat, memory, weekly. Seven agents that run your morning. Not one of them logged to the run table, because the table's allow-list never included their names. Even if they tried, the write was silently rejected.

Why it stayed hidden

A health check sees zero rows and reads it as "nothing to report." There was no way to tell "ran clean" from "never ran." So a calendar that died weeks ago, or a snapshot read that started failing, just kept not showing up.

A self-refilling graveyard

Feeders regenerated "new lead" and "going cold" alerts every 20 minutes, nobody opened the inbox page, and email items could not be actioned in the app. Nothing aged or de-duplicated them, so the queue only grew, and the one place you actually look was blind to all of it.

07 / 09
The fix · PR #740, live in prod

A freshness contract, real observability, and messages on the load.

One change, shipped and deployed today, that turns silent failure into a loud signal and puts the missing data back in front of you.

  • Messages on load. The load now surfaces owner-flagged customer messages and the unreviewed backlog, ordered with everything else by what moves money.
  • A freshness alarm. The load reads the run table and flags any agent gone dark, stale, or errored, and stamps the brief with its age. Silent staleness is now a loud line. It immediately caught a scanner that had quietly stopped running.
  • Observability on all 7 agents. Migration 106 widened the allow-list; a minimal heartbeat logger now records every run. The watchers are watched.
  • A brief that tells the truth. It merges live bookings into Today, says "calendar unavailable" honestly instead of faking an empty day, and leads with real money: chase El Rody $18,075, McCardle $12,708, Bissett $11,155.
  • An inbox that drains itself. A sweep ages out stale alerts and de-duplicates the backlog every cycle, and never drops an alert that has not fired yet.
