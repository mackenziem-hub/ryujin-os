THE BUYBACK ENGINE

Plus Ultra hires to buy back Mac's time, not just to grow

Every role at Plus Ultra exists to pull Mac out of low-value work and back into the seat only he can fill. This is the Dan Martell Buyback lens applied to the real team: Cat, Darcy, Diego, AJ, Pavanjot, and the sub crew.

  • The principle: delegate and hire to reclaim the owner's hours, then refill those hours with the work that makes the most money and lights Mac up.
  • The trap to avoid: growing the team while Mac stays buried in inbox, CRM hygiene, formatting, and routine field work.
  • The metric that matters: how cleanly Mac stays in Leadership + Production while rungs 1-4 mature to outcome-ownership.

BUYBACK RATE

Mac stops doing any task he can buy back for less than his BBR

Buyback Rate = (annual take-home / 2,000 hours) / 4. Exact comp is not on file, so run three cases and drop in the real number. The rule is the same regardless of the cell.

  • The rule: never personally do a task you can hand off at or below your BBR.
  • At the mid case ($25/hr BBR): every Delegation-quadrant task (inbox, hygiene, scheduling, formatting) is mathematically a buy. Cat and an hourly ops person clear that bar easily.
  • Run your real number: last quarter's owner draws, annualized, / 2,000, / 4. That dollar figure is the filter for every 'should I do this' call.

THE LOOP

Walk every role up the ladder until Mac owns only Leadership and Production

The engine is not a one-time sort. It is a recurring loop: as each person matures from review-everything to review-by-exception to owns-the-outcome, Mac releases another layer and refills it with higher work.

  • SOPs via the Camcorder Method: Mac records himself doing a task 3x, it becomes a Course, a Cadence, and a Checklist someone else runs.
  • Autonomy levers: 1-3-1 rule (1 problem, 3 options, 1 recommendation), a written Definition of Done, 10-80-10 (Mac briefs the first 10% and polishes the last 10%), and small spending authority so minor issues never bounce back.
  • The trust gradient: review-everything -> review-by-exception -> owns-the-outcome. A rung is done when one person owns the outcome.
  • Next move: name the rung-2 delivery owner and run Mac's real BBR number, then re-sort against it.
