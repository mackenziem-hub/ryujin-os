The winning design

HOUSE DRAGON: Ryujin stays home and hunts

Through 2026, Ryujin is Plus Ultra's proprietary AI chief of staff. Not a product. The proof it generates becomes the product later.

  1. One chat brain. api/chat.js is the only brain; the two duplicate chat endpoints die. Jarvis voice stays as the demo garnish.
  2. Delegation rule engine. Action classes: Cat approves drafted sends, only Mac approves money. The 66-draft backlog burns to zero in June.
  3. Port LOAD/SAVE into the product. Session brain, daily brief on open, save on close. The business stops running on terminal sessions.
  4. Bank-truth ledger. RBC, Financeit, and Stripe statements reconciled against the payments table that undercounts by 40 percent, with a weekly cash-retained scorecard.
  5. Proof-pack instrumentation. Speed to lead, close rate per proposal, Mac-hours per job, monthly cash retained. Auto-compiled. This is the future sales deck no competitor can copy.

What the adversarial critic caught

Four landmines nobody else priced

CASL exposure on outbound revival

Implied consent expires 6 months after an inquiry. Penalties run to $10M per violation. The only opt-out machinery in the entire API layer is reading GHL's dnd flag. A consent ledger and STOP handling come before any volume outbound.

Zero privacy or terms pages anywhere

The funnels collect PII today with no privacy policy in public/. PIPEDA scaffolding, a DPA, and a subprocessor list are launch blockers for anything sold.

Vendor terms unverified on all three dependencies

Every GHL Agency and SaaS-mode fact came from affiliate SEO blogs. Anthropic consumer-plan resale and EagleView report rebundling were never checked against actual terms.

SDE was never computed

With debits tracking deposits one to one and personal spend commingled, paper SDE may be near zero. The exit math everyone quotes hangs on a number that does not exist yet.

Only you can answer these

Five decisions the whole plan hangs on

  1. End state. A software company, Plus Ultra grown to a max-multiple exit, or roofing funds software in sequence? Every other decision forks off this one.
  2. Solo bootstrap, or not. Is taking capital or a cofounder on the table to chase a window that closes around late 2027?
  3. Corp-dev. If JobNimbus, Roofr, or Netic offered to buy or license the quote engine outright, do you sell, or is owning the platform non-negotiable?
  4. First market. Canada only (CASL and PIPEDA, smaller TAM, no TCPA) or US metros (bigger TAM, 10DLC registration, cross-border tax)? Different compliance builds.
  5. Cat's deal. She is assigned first-line support and cash-discipline enforcement in two designs. Nobody has asked her, and nobody has priced it.
