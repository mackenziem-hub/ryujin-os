The root cause

The winners were switched off

It felt like whack-a-mole because it was. The two campaigns that produced most of June's leads, Instant Estimator and Revive, had been paused. The money was running on five fresh, unproven cold-test campaigns instead.

What the account looked like

Five campaigns active. All five were June cold tests. The proven lead engines sat paused while the budget spent on creative with no track record. Pause a winner and the leads it was buying simply stop, so the next day the numbers look broken.

The alarm was noise

Meta credits a lead back to the day its ad was seen, often several days later. So the most recent week always looks lead-starved, and cost per lead always looks worse than it will finally settle at.

On watch time

Watched is not the same as converted

Video creative

  • 80 to 93% hook rate, people reach 3 seconds
  • One video held 29% of viewers to the end
  • 8 to 9 seconds average watch on the best one
  • Zero leads on $727 over 90 days

Static images

  • No watch time at all, they are images
  • $11 to $13 per lead
  • 12 and 5 leads in June from two statics
  • The cheapest leads in the whole account

The takeaway

High engagement does not predict leads here. Our videos entertain, our statics sell. Stop funding video inside the lead campaigns.

How the ads should be set up

The setup that should hold

Three lead campaigns only

Instant Estimator, Revive, and the 10CM funnel. Concentrate the budget so each ad set gets enough conversions to actually optimize.

Statics only in lead ad sets

Video is watched but not converted here. Keep video for awareness, on its own budget, never inside the lead campaigns.

Optimize for the Lead event

Every lead ad set should optimize toward the pixel Lead, not clicks or landing-page views. One suspicious $2 to $3 cost-per-result suggests at least one is set wrong. Verify it.

Retarget warm only, and only if it converts

Current retargeting is at zero leads, so it stays paused until it is rebuilt off real Instant Estimator visitors and video viewers.

12 / 13
The cadence

Judge on signed jobs, review weekly

Judge on 30-day lead cost and signed jobs

Never a 7-day cost-per-lead number. That window is structurally noisy because of the attribution lag.

Review weekly, not daily

Change creative only when a 30-day window genuinely shifts. No more recommend-a-change-then-ask-what-happened.

Retune the alarm

Compare settled windows, days 8 to 14 against 15 to 21, so the monitor stops firing off un-backfilled days and stops triggering changes.

This is the part that stops the whack-a-mole

Set the structure, then leave it alone for a week. The two resumed campaigns will re-enter Meta's learning phase for three to five days, so a little volatility this week is expected and is not a reason to touch anything.
