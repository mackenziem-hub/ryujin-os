State of play

Healthy on the surface. Fragile underneath.

Three live ad campaigns, a strong proposal engine, four jobs in motion, a new Revive margin stream coming online. A $242K signed book behind us. The engine runs.

The crack under it

The same failure mode repeats in every part of the business: high value work built to 90 percent, then parked on one unmade decision or one unsent message. Momentum and cash leak at the last inch.

03 / 18
The pattern

Built to 90 percent, parked on one action

  • The Revive ad: finished, captioned, branded. Parked because it has no music bed.
  • The 28 lead follow up list: curated and tiered. Parked, never sent.
  • The Revive nurture pipeline: built, 11 stages, 3 workflows. Parked, never switched on.
  • The June promo: drafted. Parked on two answers from Ben.
  • Stalled proposals worth six figures: written, sitting, going cold.

None of these need building. They need one decision or one send. That is the whole disease.

05 / 18
Where it leaks

One of everything, and no backup

  • One sprayer. Diego scores and sprays every Revive job. No backup, no second sprayer trained.
  • One subcontractor. Ryan installs every replacement, currently unsigned and uninsured.
  • One channel for payroll. Crew hours live only in Diego's Messenger, readable only by Mac.
  • One owner. Sales, pricing, dispatch, and the build all queue on Mac. He is the master single point.

Why it matters for 10x

You cannot multiply a business by 10 through single points of failure. Each one becomes the wall the moment volume climbs.

06 / 18
The diagnosis

"What happens if I double ad spend tomorrow?"

Hormozi's one question for finding the real bottleneck. If the answer is clean 2x revenue, you are short on demand. If the answer is chaos, you are short on delivery.

Our honest answer

Chaos. The funnel already makes more leads than one sprayer can touch, and tagged leads are sitting cold because the nurture never turns on. More ads would not make more money. They would make a bigger backlog.

07 / 18
The one constraint

Demand already runs 10x past delivery

About 195 warm leads a month against roughly 20 install days for one sprayer. The gap is the constraint. More ads widen it.
08 / 18
The counterintuitive part

Do not buy more leads

The instinct is to pour fuel on marketing. The numbers say the opposite. Demand is already 10x ahead of what we can install.

The reframe

You do not 10x by selling more Revive jobs. You 10x by manufacturing spray-days you currently cannot make. Activate the dead pipeline, lock one price, clone the sprayer, then point the proven offer at a denser map. The leads are not the problem.

10 / 18
The Grand Slam offer

One named offer, not a service line

  • The value. Add 5 years to your roof, done in a day, no tear off, no dumpster, for a fraction of a $15 to $25K replacement.
  • The risk reversal. Free Shingle Death Score assessment. If Revive cannot save it, we say so and credit it toward a replacement. You cannot lose by booking the look.
  • Real scarcity. Limited June assessment slots per town, tied to actual capacity, so demand never outruns the one sprayer.
  • The close. Two options only, Revive or Replace. Never a lower tier fallback.

12 / 18
Operation Revive Atlantic Canada

Same offer, denser map

Widening from Greater Moncton out through Shediac, Sussex, and Sackville is the right move. It is geographic expansion of one proven offer, not a new product. That is watering the lawn, not jumping the fence.

The one tax on it

A wider radius taxes the one sprayer. Sussex is about 70 minutes out, Sackville about 50, and they do not chain. The rule: never send Diego on a single far job. Batch 2 or 3 in one town and run them as a route, or the drive eats the margin.

13 / 18
Plug the bucket first

Three leaks that turn 10x into a bleed

  • The dead pipeline. Built, never enrolled, leaking leads at 1x today. Watch the 2 Looms and switch it on. 20 minutes, free, highest ROI on the board.
  • The unsettled price. Three numbers floating means every quote routes through Mac. The $167 table fixes it.
  • Margin discipline. At 13 to 16 percent real net there is nothing to give away. Hold price, add bonuses, never discount.
14 / 18
The actual 10x lever

Revive scales with sprayers, not ad spend

The dollar ceiling on Revive is spray-days available, and today that equals one calendar. Until a second sprayer exists, "Atlantic Canada" is aspirational.

The move

Cross train a second in house applicator, AJ or Pavanjot, on scoring and spraying. This is a hiring and training problem, not a marketing one. It is the single thing that makes the 10x math real.
