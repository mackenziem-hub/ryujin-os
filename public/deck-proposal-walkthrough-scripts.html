The spine · same five beats every time

Run the same five beats on all seven and they all sound like you.

  • 1 · Open Greet by name, thank them, one line on where the roof lands. "Here is the straight read."
  • 2 · The read Scroll the inspection photos. Point at three or four things you actually saw. Plain words, name the part.
  • 3 · Straight talk Say what it means in one honest sentence. If it is end of life, say end of life.
  • 4 · The plan + three ways Same install on all three. Walk the ladder. Point them at one.
  • 5 · Close No push. "Have a look and call or text me directly." Leave both doors open.
Beats stay fixed · the middle (the read) is what changes per roof
03 / 13
How to sound · driveway, not brochure

Talk like you are standing in their driveway pointing at the roof.

  • Slow down You know this cold. Let the photo sit on screen a beat before you talk.
  • Name it "The granules are gone here." "That valley holds water." Concrete, no fluff.
  • Own the call Tell them which tier you would put on your own house, and why.
  • Drop it in Say it once, early: "third-generation roofing, I sign my own name to every proposal."
  • No hard close Never "act now." End on "have a look, call or text me."
  • Stay you No bashing anyone, no scare talk. Just the straight read and the options.

Two plays that carry five of the seven

You only really have two scripts here. Learn these and the rest is names.

  • Play A · the turn-down Donald and Jose. You said no to the easy money on the spray. That builds more trust than any pitch.
  • Run it "Not a fit, I won't sell you a spray that won't hold." Show the evidence. Then offer replacement as the honest path. Do not apologize for it.
  • Play B · system not panels Catherine and Desiree. You are selling time and warranty backing, not steel.
  • Run it Visualizer first so they see their house. Then walk the 20 to 25 to 30 year ladder in plain terms. "What you are buying is time."
Why it works Both plays put you on the customer's side of the table before any price shows up. That is the whole brand in one move.
