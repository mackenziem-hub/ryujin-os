The Premise

What this sequence is for

A homeowner downloads the Roof Rejuvenation PDF. They've heard the word "rejuvenation" and they want to know if it applies to their roof. The job is two parallel things: (1) qualify whether the roof is a rejuvenation candidate or a replacement candidate, and (2) get a Spray Feasibility Inspection booked on Diego's calendar.

The disqualifier path matters

Active leaks or missing shingles means rejuvenation can't perform. Those leads need a regular inspection, not a spray feasibility. The disqualifier reroute is a feature, not a leak in the funnel. A lead routed correctly to repair is still a win.

The 3 things that decide if rejuvenation works for your roof

Email
Subject: The 3 things that decide if rejuvenation works for your roof
Hi {first_name}, Quick follow-up. There are really only three things that decide whether rejuvenation is the right call for a roof: 1. Shingle integrity. The shingles need to still be flexible and mostly intact. If they're cracking or crumbling, rejuvenation won't bring them back. 2. No active leaks. Spray treatment seals and rejuvenates the surface, but it doesn't fix existing leaks underneath. Roofs with leaks need a different conversation first. 3. Plans to stay. Rejuvenation pays off best if you're staying in the home another 5+ years. If you're selling next spring, a full replacement may serve you better at resale. If all three apply to you, you're a strong candidate. Free spray feasibility inspection takes 45 min and gives you a yes/no plus a quote on the spot: {calendar_link} Mac
