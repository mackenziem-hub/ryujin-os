Why metal

What you are really buying is time.

Asphalt is a 30-year decision. Metal is measured in generations. Here is what that buys you.

A roof you install once

Exposed-fastener ribbed steel runs 40 to 50 years. Concealed-fastener snap-lock runs 50-plus. For most homes this is the last roof you ever buy, where asphalt comes back around every few decades.

It sheds snow and ice

A steep 12/12 metal face sheds snow load instead of holding it. Less weight sitting on your structure, fewer ice dams forcing water back under the edge.

Non-combustible

Steel does not catch and does not feed a fire. It is one of the calmer materials to have over your head when an ember lands.

Near-zero maintenance

No granules in the gutter, no curling tabs, no moss. Once it is on and flashed right, a metal roof asks almost nothing of you for decades.

The honest part

Why do this now

Real talk. If nothing changes on this roof between now and next spring, there is no rush, and I would rather you feel sure. But the steep faces on this house are unforgiving once water finds a way in. Doing it now means you choose the timeline instead of the weather choosing it for you, and you lock in today's pricing and your preferred week before the spring calendar fills.

Conditions and assumptions

  • Standard and European Clay both install over your existing shingles, so they assume a single-layer, flat roof with sound decking. If we find a second layer or rotted deck, the right fit is Premium, which tears off to the deck.
  • Premium exposes the deck so any issues are caught and addressed during the job.
  • Any unforeseen deck rot beyond the spec is handled at $80 per sheet, with photo documentation shown to you and approved before we replace.
  • Lead time from acceptance is typically 4 to 6 weeks for material delivery and scheduling.
