Purpose

One Document. One Source of Truth.

This handbook defines the outside sales position at Plus Ultra Roofing — what the company owes the rep, what the rep owes the company, and how disagreements get resolved. It exists so neither side has to negotiate the same question twice. Where this document conflicts with prior verbal agreements, this document governs from the effective date forward; nothing earned before that date is reduced.

Section 2

Lead Sources & Routing

Every lead in Plus Ultra falls into one of four categories. Routing and commission tier are determined at the moment of lead creation in GHL.

2.1 Lead categories

CategorySourceOwner of nurtureOwner of close
A — Self-SourcedDoor-knocked, walk-up, repeat customer who contacted rep directly, true rep referralRepRep
B — Company-Supplied (Hot)Inbound that company VA has nurtured to "ready for inspection" stageCompany VA (Cat)Rep
C — Company-Supplied (Cold)10CM PDF download, IE quote with no follow-up, Meta lead form, retargeting clickCompany VA (Cat)Not rep — assigned to rep only after VA promotes to Hot
D — Service / RepairInbound asking for a repair or small fixService person (AJ or future hire)Service person — NOT outside sales

2.2 Routing rules

  • Cold inbound (Category C) does NOT go to the rep. Cat owns the nurture cadence (SMS + email drip) until the lead either (a) books an inspection or (b) goes dormant past 90 days. Booked inspection promotes the lead to Category B and assigns it to the rep.
  • Repairs (Category D) do NOT go to the rep. All inbound repair requests route to the service person. If the service visit reveals a full-roof opportunity, it escalates to the rep — see Section 4.4.
  • If the rep arrives at a Category B inspection and discovers it's actually a repair (under $2,500 threshold), rep does NOT quote on-site. Rep photographs, takes notes, tells customer "our service tech will follow up within 48 hours," and hands off to service. Rep gets a flat $50 site-visit spiff for the courtesy visit.
  • Mac may override routing at any time by written note in GHL; verbal overrides do not bind commission.

2.3 Lead-source classification dispute

If the rep believes a lead was misclassified (e.g. a Category B that should have been Category A because it was a referral from their customer), the dispute resolution is: does the GHL contact note at original sale document a lineage to the rep's prior customer? If yes, marketing commission applies (Section 3.3). If not, no.

Non-negotiable: if it's not in writing in GHL, it doesn't count. Both sides have responsibility to keep notes current.

Company-Supplied

How "collected revenue" is calculated: total contract value minus HST minus any chargebacks (cancellation, customer credit, warranty rework cost). It is NOT "hard cost" — historical Plus Ultra commission has been calculated on hard cost; going forward, commission is on collected revenue (pre-HST, post-discount, post-chargeback) so the math is transparent and matches industry standard.

HST is not commissionable. Discounts applied to win the deal reduce commissionable revenue. Free Platinum upgrade promo etc. — commission is on what the customer actually paid.

3.2 Marketing Commission (2nd-Generation Referral Bonus)

+5%
Marketing commission Stacks on top of Tier A or Tier B primary on any deal traceable to a customer the rep originally sold. Maximum total commission on any single deal: 15% (Tier A + 5% marketing).
  • Lineage requirement: the original customer's GHL contact must have been notated at the time of original sale identifying the rep as originator. If lineage is not documented in GHL at time of original sale, marketing commission does NOT apply, regardless of memory or verbal claim.
  • One generation only. A referral of a referral does not chain back to the original rep.

3.3 Site-Visit Spiff (Repair Handoff)

$50 flat when rep attends a Category B inspection that turns out to be a sub-$2,500 repair and hands off to the service person without quoting.
  • Does NOT apply if rep quotes the repair anyway (rep is not authorized to quote sub-$2,500 work — see Section 4.4).
  • Paid monthly with primary commission.

3.4 When Commission Is Earned vs. Paid

  • Earned: at signed contract + cleared deposit.
  • Paid: within 7 days of full customer payment clearing the company account.
  • Partial payments do NOT trigger partial commission. Full collection only.
  • Customers on financing (FinanceIT etc.) — commission is paid when the financing disbursement clears, not when the customer signs the financing agreement.

3.5 Chargebacks

Commission is reversed (or deducted from the next pay cycle) under any of:

  • Customer cancels before crew mobilizes — full reversal.
  • Customer cancels after material order placed — commission reversed minus any cancellation fee retained by company.
  • Job loses money below 10% net margin due to scope under-quoted by rep at time of sale (not due to production overrun) — commission reduced pro-rata to bring company net to 10%.
  • Customer chargeback / non-payment / collections — commission reversed if reversed at company level.
  • Warranty rework cost exceeds 5% of contract value — commission reduced by the rework cost amount.
Rep is informed in writing of any chargeback within 7 days of the triggering event.

3.6 Past Commission

All commission paid prior to the effective date of this handbook is honored as paid. No retroactive reversal, no clawback. The terms in this section apply only to deals signed on or after the effective date.

Section 4

Activity Ownership

The default rule: the rep is a closer + relationship-builder, not a project manager or office worker. The activities below define what the rep is responsible for and what they're not.

4.1 Rep Owns

  • Inspecting the roof (climbing, photographing, measuring) — every quoted job
  • Documenting damage scope in GHL (photos uploaded same day)
  • Pricing the deal using the company price book in Ryujin — no off-book pricing
  • Generating the proposal PDF from the company template
  • Presenting and closing the proposal in-home or via approved follow-up channel
  • Collecting the signed contract + deposit at signing
  • One courtesy visit during install (intro to crew, confirm customer comfortable)
  • Asking for review + referral within 7 days of job completion
  • Maintaining GHL hygiene: stage moves, contact notes, photos uploaded, every interaction logged

4.2 Rep Does NOT Own

  • Material ordering — production / Mac
  • Crew scheduling — production / Mac
  • Final invoicing — office (Cat / Mac)
  • Final payment collection — office, except where rep relationship is the most effective collection lever (rep then assists, not owns)
  • Warranty registration — office
  • Insurance supplements / claim negotiations — Mac (until claim specialist hired)
  • On-site QC during install — production / Mac
  • Change-order pricing — Mac signs off, rep re-presents to customer for re-sign

4.3 Pricing Authority

  • All pricing comes from the Ryujin price book. Period.
  • Rep may NOT freelance pricing on-site. If a customer asks for a discount, rep may apply company-approved promotions only (currently: Free Platinum Upgrade, see promo bank for time-bounded offers).
  • Discounts beyond the approved promotion bank require Mac sign-off in writing (text, email, GHL note).
  • Quoting outside the price book = the deal is not commissionable until Mac re-prices and re-issues.
Off-book pricing without authorization is grounds for immediate termination after one written warning (Section 11.2).

4.4 Repair Carve-Out

  • Rep does not quote repairs under $2,500.
  • Rep does not take inbound repair calls.
  • If a Category B inspection turns out to be a repair → rep takes photos, hands off to service, collects $50 site-visit spiff (Section 3.3).
  • If a service-tech repair visit reveals a full-roof opportunity → service tech flags in GHL, lead routes to rep as a Category B (since service tech did the warm-up). Service tech receives a $50 finder's spiff if rep closes; rep receives Tier B commission.

Section 8

Performance Review & Improvement

8.1 Review Cadence

  • Monthly: 30-min check-in with Mac. Review pipeline, activity metrics, SLA performance, customer feedback. Not formal — collaborative.
  • Quarterly: formal review. Activity vs. floor, close rate, revenue produced, customer review/referral count, written summary signed by both parties.
  • Annual: handbook review + comp structure review. Either party may propose changes.

8.2 Performance Improvement Path (PIP)

Triggered by:

  • Two consecutive months below activity floor
  • Three SLA misses in a calendar month
  • Pattern of customer complaints or quality escalations
  • Repeated off-book pricing

PIP structure:

  1. Written notice from Mac identifying the issue + the metric to improve + timeframe (typically 30 days).
  2. Mid-PIP check-in at day 15.
  3. Day 30 review: improvement met → PIP closes; not met → either second 30-day extension, role change, or termination per Section 11.
The PIP is intended as a clarification tool, not a paper trail to fire. If we need to use it, we use it honestly.
Section 9

Mac's Obligations to the Rep

This is the half of the agreement most companies leave out. Plus Ultra commits to the following:

  1. Lead flow target: at least 4 qualified Category B handoffs per week, averaged monthly. If lead flow falls below this for two consecutive months, the rep may renegotiate Tier B commission upward or shift effort to self-gen without penalty.
  2. Pricing engine maintained: Ryujin price book current, accurate, and reflects company target margin. If the rep uses the price book correctly and the deal still loses money, that is NOT a chargeback against the rep.
  3. Proposal templates current: branded, accurate, and easy to customize.
  4. Production timeliness: signed jobs scheduled within 30 days unless customer requests later, and rep is informed of schedule.
  5. Commission paid on time: within 7 days of customer full-payment clear. Late commission for any reason → rep is informed in writing same day with expected pay date.
  6. Decision turnaround: Mac responds to rep's escalations (pricing exceptions, supplement asks, customer disputes) within 24 business hours.
  7. No unilateral comp changes: any change to commission structure requires 30 days written notice and applies only to deals signed after notice period.
  8. Tool access: GHL + Ryujin + email + phone forwarding remain functional. Outage > 24 hrs → company expedites fix.
  9. Customer-facing materials: truck wrap, business cards, branded shirts, yard signs supplied at company expense (annual budget).
  10. Backup for time off: rep may take reasonable PTO. Cat or Mac covers in-pipeline customer comms during absence; commission on in-flight deals stays with rep.
Section 10

Lead Ownership, Confidentiality, Non-Solicit

10.1 Lead Ownership

  • All leads, contacts, and customer relationships generated through Plus Ultra channels (marketing, signage, referrals from prior Plus Ultra customers, inbound calls to the company number) are company property.
  • "Self-generated" is defined narrowly: rep brought the contact's name + number to the company with zero marketing assist. A door-knock at a house that responded to a Plus Ultra yard sign three doors down is NOT self-gen.
  • All contact info, photos, scope notes, pricing, and proposal documents are company IP.

10.2 Confidentiality

  • Pricing book, supplier terms, customer list, financial information, and internal SOPs are confidential. Rep may not share with competitors, family-members-in-the-trade, or third parties without written permission.
  • Customer photos and project details may be shared on rep's personal social media only with company branding intact and customer consent.

10.3 Non-Solicit (12 months post-termination)

For 12 months following the end of this engagement, rep agrees not to:

  • Solicit Plus Ultra customers (active or completed within prior 24 months) for roofing, exterior, or related construction services.
  • Solicit Plus Ultra employees, subcontractors, or other reps to leave the company.
  • Use confidential information (customer list, pricing, supplier contacts) in any competing capacity.
Note: this is a non-solicit, not a non-compete. Rep is free to work in roofing in NB, including for competitors, after termination. They simply cannot poach the customer base or staff. This is enforceable in New Brunswick when reasonable in scope and time, and 12 months is reasonable.
Section 11

Classification, Termination, Pipeline Transition

11.1 Classification

Rep is engaged as an Independent Sales Consultant (1099-equivalent contractor). This means:

  • Rep is responsible for own income tax remittance, GST/HST registration if applicable, CPP self-contribution.
  • Rep is not entitled to vacation pay, statutory holidays, employment insurance, or company benefits.
  • Rep operates with autonomy on schedule, method, and territory within the bounds of this handbook.
  • Rep may not represent themselves as an employee of Plus Ultra to customers or third parties.

If at any point the company chooses to convert the rep to a W2 employee, that change requires its own written agreement with revised comp structure (typically lower base % + benefits).

11.2 Termination

Either party may terminate this engagement with 14 days written notice (text, email, or letter all acceptable).

Immediate termination (no notice period) by company is allowed for:
  • Off-book pricing without authorization (after one written warning)
  • Misrepresentation to customers
  • Theft, fraud, or misappropriation
  • Conduct on jobsites or with customers that creates legal/brand liability (drugs, alcohol on the job, harassment, threatening behavior)
  • Material breach of confidentiality or non-solicit

11.3 Pipeline at Termination

  • Signed + deposit cleared + customer paid in full before termination date: commission paid normally.
  • Signed + deposit cleared + customer not yet paid in full at termination: commission paid on collection, on normal schedule, regardless of whether rep is still engaged.
  • Quoted but not signed at termination: lead reverts to company, no commission. If the lead signs within 30 days of termination on a quote the rep delivered, the rep receives 50% of normal commission on collection, in good faith.
  • Inspections completed but not yet quoted at termination: lead reverts to company, no commission.
  • In-pipeline cold leads (Category C nurture): company property, no claim by rep regardless of how they were classified.

11.4 Final Pay

  • Final pay (any earned-but-unpaid commission as of termination date, on the schedule above) is the company's only obligation.
  • No severance, no PTO payout, no continuation of benefits (none existed under contractor classification).
  • Rep returns: company-supplied phone (if applicable), branded clothing, yard signs, demo materials, Ryujin/GHL access revoked within 24 hours of termination notice (or earlier if cause).
Section 12

Code of Conduct

The reputation of Plus Ultra Roofing is the most valuable asset both parties have. Conduct that puts it at risk is the fastest path to termination, regardless of sales performance.

12.1 With Customers

  • Honest representation of scope, timeline, materials, and price. No exaggeration of damage, no "scare close" tactics.
  • Respect of property — no smoking on customer property, no littering, no unsupervised wandering of the home.
  • Professional appearance: clean clothing (branded preferred), no visible intoxication, no offensive language.
  • Respect of customer's "no" — when a customer declines, the rep leaves a business card and follows up at company-approved cadence (no harassment).

12.2 On Jobsites

Drugs and alcohol are prohibited on any Plus Ultra jobsite. This includes the rep's own consumption regardless of off-duty status while on or near customer property.
  • Crew conduct concerns are escalated to Mac immediately.
  • Photo + video capture of jobsites for social media requires customer consent.

12.3 In Communications

  • Customer-facing communication (text, email, social, in-person) represents the Plus Ultra brand. Tone is professional, warm, direct.
  • No badmouthing competitors. Two-tier framing is the company standard for competitive pricing situations.
  • No badmouthing past customers, current customers, the company, or coworkers in front of clients or third parties.

12.4 Online Presence

  • Rep's personal social media may discuss roofing work, but anything mentioning Plus Ultra by name or showing branded materials must align with company standards. Mac may request takedown of any post that creates legal or brand risk; rep complies within 24 hours.
Section 13

Dispute Resolution

When the rep and Mac disagree:

  1. First: check this handbook. If the situation is covered, the handbook governs.
  2. Second: check GHL + written records. If the GHL note doesn't say it, it didn't happen.
  3. Third: sit down for a 15-minute conversation. No texting back-and-forth on contested issues.
  4. Fourth: if still unresolved, escalate to a written-decision request — rep submits the question in writing, Mac responds in writing within 5 business days. That written response governs.
  5. Final: if either party feels the handbook itself is wrong, the annual review (Section 8) is the place to renegotiate. Until renegotiated, the existing terms govern.
