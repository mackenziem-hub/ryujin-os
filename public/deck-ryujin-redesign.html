The mandate

Four things are wrong. This fixes all four.

Too many pages

Around ninety-five navigable screens. Three competing cockpits plus a game mode. One pillar alone carries thirteen sub-pages.

Sprawl

Every page too dense

KPI rail, then a stat row, then brief cards, then a timeline, all stacked on one screen. We kept adding sections and never took any away.

Density

Feels generic

It reads like every other contractor dashboard. None of the dragon, none of the storm, none of the thing that makes it ours.

Off-brand

Dashboard-first

You hunt through tiles to do one thing. The model should lead with the agent, and the agent should do the thing.

Wrong model

04 / 15
The direction

One direction: The Summon

The agent is the home screen and the primary way work happens. You state intent, the dragon summons the exact view you need, scoped to that one thing. Dashboards stop being destinations and become surfaces the agent conjures. Ninety-five screens collapse to one cockpit, nine workspaces, and a handful of system pages.

The conversation is the home

The app cold-opens to the dragon, one input, and a few live chips drawn from today's real state. Not a wall of numbers.

P1

Views are summoned, not stored as pages

Every old page becomes a state the cockpit renders in place, keyed to what you asked for. No ten-deep page trees.

P2

One primary object per screen

One hero object: a job, a quote, today's money. Secondary data is demoted to the dock or revealed on demand.

P3

Three modes are the only depth control

Agent, Interactive, Advanced. The same toggle governs depth everywhere. Density becomes opt-in, never the default.

P4

Nine pillars, nine workspaces

Each pillar is one mode-aware workspace. Sub-pages become tabs and states, not separate pages.

P5

Storm-calm aesthetic

Dark navy water, teal-mint as the single action accent, the dragon as a persistent, reactive presence. Calm by default.

Principles 1 + 2

Agent-first, made literal

You state intent. The dragon surfaces and summons the one object you need, with the next action already staged.

"Schedule Cornhill" pulls the single job onto the stage with the next open crew slot proposed in the dock. You confirm or adjust. The agent did the cross-referencing you used to do by hand, across four pages.

The model in one line

Intent in. Agent resolves. One object out, with the next move pre-staged as a chip you confirm.

06 / 15
Principle 3

One object per screen

Today, Finance stacks four card types at once and makes you scan for the one number you wanted. Tomorrow it shows that number, and offers the next move.

Same data. Everything else in Finance stays one summon away, not stacked on screen competing for your attention.
07 / 15
Principle 4

Three modes, everywhere

The same toggle governs depth on the cockpit and inside every pillar. You choose how much you see. Calm is the default; density is something you opt into.

This replaces today's inconsistency: layer-cards on Finance, a hub on Sales, nothing on Production. One mechanism, applied uniformly.

Lock it in

What you lock today

  • The direction is The Summon. Agent-first, radically consolidated, decluttered, on-brand.
  • The cockpit layout. One home, dragon-led, live chips, no KPI wall.
  • Three modes as the only depth control. Agent, Interactive, Advanced, applied everywhere.
  • One workspace per pillar. Production is the template the other eight follow.
  • Navy and teal-mint, one accent, dragon-led. Retire the per-pillar color chaos.

Annotate any of these in place. When all five feel right, we lock and move to the build plan.
