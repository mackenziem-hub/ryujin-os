What the field tells us

Do less and you own the field. The scoped AI absorbs everything else.

Across every platform, crews abandon apps for bloat. 80% of software features go unused, and the tools crews adopt on day one win by doing less. AccuLynx, JobNimbus and Dataforma all lost the field the same way: clunky mobile, too many taps, too many fields. Your four-tab instinct is the strategy, not a compromise.

The multi-role move is the danger zone, and the fix is exact: scope each role into its own view, never thicken the crew screen, and let the scoped AI carry the long tail. That is the one thing ServiceTitan cannot do and you can.

The architecture · RBAC, one codebase

One app gated by role beats five apps. Collapse to four roles.

ServiceTitan, Jobber and Housecall Pro all ship ONE app and gate features by role, never separate apps per person. You do the same: Owner, EA, Crew, and Sub, plus a media-only Installer link for a sub's people. New people drop into the lightest tier that fits, and you elevate by exception. The rule that keeps it safe: the client decides which tabs to paint, the server decides which rows to return. Hiding a tab is cosmetic, scoping the data is the real boundary.

The keystone · the inbox that dispatches

The Inbox is the real dispatcher: the AI reads it, a rules table routes it, you confirm the writes.

The trust trick from the research: the AI only classifies the message (what it is, which job, how urgent). A deterministic routing table decides who gets pinged and what fires. The model never freelances who or what. That table is the same one your auto-triggers feed, so the AI genuinely sits on top of the triggers. Reads run on their own, writes propose then wait for a yes. Your confirmation-first doctrine, baked in.

Signal, not noise

Tier it, digest it, escalate it, so nobody mutes the app in week one.

A 5 to 6 person crew drowns fast if every event pings everyone. The notification engine sorts by priority, batches the rest into digests, dedupes repeats, respects quiet hours, and chases an answer when one is needed.

The plumbing · already in place

Twilio is already wired, and Canada means no registration to fight.

The DM-over-SMS path is mostly built. lib/sms.js sends direct, every teammate already has their own Ryujin number, and posting a message already fans out over SMS. For threads we use Twilio's Conversations API, which bridges in-app chat and SMS inside one thread, so someone who never opens the app still gets the text and their reply lands back in the thread.

The unlock: Canada-to-Canada texting does NOT need the US A2P 10DLC registration that blocks most projects for weeks. You use your existing numbers now. The only homework is STOP/HELP handling and a consent record (CASL). Cost at six people is rounding error. 10DLC only ever applies if you start texting US numbers.
