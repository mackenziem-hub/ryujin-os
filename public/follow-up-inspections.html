The Premise

What this sequence is for

A cold lead grabbed the 10 Costly Mistakes PDF. They downloaded a guide, not a quote. They haven't shown commercial intent yet. Pushing them into a hard sales cycle now burns the relationship. The job is to deliver value, build credibility over 3 weeks, and let the inspection ask land naturally.

Why 22 days, not 14

IE leads saw a number, they're closer to commercial. 10CM leads are still reading. Longer cadence, lighter pressure, more value-forward touches before the soft close.

Global Rules

The lines that don't move

Send windows

  • SMS: 9 AM to 7 PM contact local time, 7 days a week
  • Email: 9 AM to 11 AM contact local time, 7 days a week

Copy rules

  • No em dashes. Use commas or sentence breaks.
  • No "just" anywhere. Direct questions only.
  • Auto-footer ("Reply STOP to opt out") is location-level. Do NOT add to message bodies.

Stop conditions · every step

  • Inbound SMS reply → move to Client Responded → notify Mac → stop sequence
  • DND flag enabled → notify Mac → stop sequence
  • Inspection booked (calendar or manual stage move) → stop sequence

Use GHL's built-in "Stop on Response" feature. No custom reply branches.

On trigger · before any wait

  • Assign contact to Mackenzie Mazerolle
  • Add to Mack's Pipeline at stage New Lead
  • Tag: 10cm-download · Source: 10 Costly Mistakes PDF
  • Internal notification to Mac: subject "New 10CM Lead — {first_name}" with name, phone, email, form data

Hey {first_name}, three things I get asked on most inspections: 1. "Can my roof be repaired or does it need full replacement?" Depends on age, material, and what's underneath. We always check decking before recommending. 2. "How long should it actually last?" 20 to 25 years for asphalt in Atlantic Canada if installed right. The "right" part is the catch. 3. "What's the difference between your warranty and the manufacturer warranty?" Manufacturer covers the shingles. Workmanship warranty covers the install. The install is what fails 80% of the time.
