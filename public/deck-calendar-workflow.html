Layer 1 · Immediate

Make Monday safe first

A buffer setting alone will not solve geography. GHL does not know that Sackville to the next town is forty minutes. For Monday specifically, fix it by hand.

Monday, by hand

Split the two Monday inspections into AM and PM by area, or assign one to AJ and one to Diego so they run in parallel instead of one person driving between them.

That spacing keeps any two bookings at least two hours apart, which absorbs a normal cross-town drive. This is the answer to Cat's question about a preferred buffer time.

05 / 09
Layer 2 · Structure

A round-robin that plugs in both crews

Convert the inspection calendar to a GHL Round Robin team calendar with two members, AJ and Diego. GHL then distributes bookings between them and only offers slots that are actually open.

Prerequisite, and it is Cat's task

Connect each of AJ and Diego's Automator or Google calendars to GHL. That link is what lets round-robin check their real day and avoid double-booking. This is the "make AJ and Diego's calendars ready" item. Without it, round-robin will book over them.

The backfill crew stays out of it

The Monday trial hire is production, not inspections. His whole purpose is to free AJ and Diego up to take inspections, so the round-robin stays just the two of them.

06 / 09
Layer 3 · Routing

One smart calendar beats four calendars, for now

Rather than build four separate calendars on day one, add a service-type field to the booking form and drive the slot length and follow-up off that field. This covers Cat's options 1 through 4 without the upfront build.

Service typeSlot length
Roof inspection60 min
Skylight or repair check45 min
Warranty or follow-up visit30 min

Split into separate calendars only if one type starts crowding the others out. Revisit in a month once we can see the real mix.

07 / 09
Bug · Notifications

Two emails per booking has one root cause

Cat is seeing a general services-call notice and a separate inspection-booked notice for the same appointment. That is two notification sources firing on one event.

Almost always

The calendar's own built-in booking notification, plus a separate Automator workflow with a "Customer Booked Appointment" trigger. Both fire, so you get two emails.

What Cat checks

  1. In Automator, find every workflow with a Customer Booked Appointment or Appointment Status trigger pointed at the services calendar.
  2. Check the calendar's own Notifications tab.
  3. Keep one internal-notification source (a single workflow is best, so it is controllable and brandable) and switch the others off.

Same single-trigger discipline as the Instant Estimator fix. One trigger, nothing double-fires.
