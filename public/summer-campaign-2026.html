The Thesis

Why this summer, and why this combo

Summer 2026 is a one-time launch window. GoNano is new in NB, Plus Ultra is the first dealer in the Moncton corridor to carry it, and we're going into peak roofing season with a product that extends roofs by 10-15 years at a fraction of replacement cost. We can either drift into the season and rely on inbound leads, or we run a coordinated campaign that establishes Plus Ultra as the rejuvenation authority before competitors notice.

And one thing working against us

The product is unfamiliar. Homeowners and commercial property managers in NB don't know what nanotechnology roof treatment is. This is the entire campaign challenge in one sentence — education is the bottleneck, not lead volume or price.

Every campaign play below is designed to do one of two jobs: (1) prove the technology is real, or (2) put it in front of the audience most ready to act. We do both, in parallel, for 4 months.

04 / 16
Competitive Landscape

Who else is doing this in Greater Moncton

One sentence: nobody is, yet. That's the entire competitive thesis.

Rejuvenation Space · Greater Moncton

Empty. Plus Ultra is first.

  • No Roof Maxx dealer in NB or Atlantic Canada (Roof Maxx has 300+ US dealers but no Atlantic Canada presence as of May 2026)
  • No competing nanotech roof product being sold in Greater Moncton
  • No local roofer marketing rejuvenation as a service — Ragnarok, BelVue, Evolve, All Angles Covered, TriForce all sell replacement only
  • NanoSeal NB exclusive dealer status = product moat for the partner who locks down territory
Replacement Roofing · The incumbent space

Crowded, commoditized, growing

  • Direct competitors: Ragnarok, All Angles Covered, BelVue, Evolve Construction, TriForce
  • Plus Ultra's edge: CertainTeed ShingleMaster, drone + CompanyCam workflow, 119:1 ROAS, $113K signed 2026 YTD on a small crew
  • Where rejuvenation changes the game: we stop competing on tear-off labor and start selling a category nobody else has
  • Cross-sell: rejuvenation leads pull through to replacement quotes on the 17-27% of roofs we score as ineligible

The 12-18 month window

Roof Maxx is actively recruiting Canadian dealers. They will arrive in Atlantic Canada within 12-18 months — possibly sooner. The launch window for Plus Ultra to establish category leadership and lock APHL + similar institutional accounts is now through summer 2027. After that, we're defending market share against a Roof Maxx franchise spending five-figures per month on local lead-gen.

Strategic implication

Move fast, lock the institutional relationships (APHL portfolio + similar non-profits + property managers), and use the summer 2026 launch to make "Plus Ultra = roof rejuvenation in NB" the default search-term association. Brand SEO + earned media this summer is more valuable than ad spend later.

Play A · HOT Segment

APHL Tara Court Close + Portfolio Expansion

May – September $60-200K revenue ~3 deals target

Tara Court is the door. APHL has 6 buildings here, plus Tannery Court (Moncton), 72 Whitney, 8 Terrance, and a Saint John / Fredericton commercial portfolio. Land one pilot well, the rest follows. This isn't a campaign — it's account management.

Mechanics

  • Step 1 — Send the Tara Court proposal to Kevin Chase after Ben confirms pricing
  • Step 2 — Book a 30-min walkthrough with Kevin + property manager. Walk the worst building. Sell Option B (recommended).
  • Step 3 — Close. Schedule June/July application window.
  • Step 4 — Document the work (Loom + drone + CompanyCam) as the master case study.
  • Step 5 — Within 30 days of completion, reach back to Kevin with the same proposal format for the next APHL property.

Investment & KPIs

  • Cost: Time only. Plus Ultra labor + NanoSeal product (margin built into the quote).
  • Primary KPI: Tara Court signed by June 1, 2026
  • Secondary KPI: 2nd APHL property quoted by July 31
  • Multiplier: Tara Court completion = the case study that powers Plays B, C, D, E

Risks

  • Board decision cycle takes 2-4 weeks — start now
  • If a competitor reaches APHL first with a Roof Maxx alternative, the exclusivity story weakens

Play B · WARM Segment

Past Client Re-engagement Sequence

May – August ~200 records $20-40K revenue

Plus Ultra has signed customers who already trust the brand. Many had asphalt installed 4-10 years ago — the prime sweet spot for Revive / Fortify. They're the lowest-cost-to-close segment Plus Ultra has access to.

Mechanics

  • Segment the CRM by install date: 0-3 yrs (Fortify), 4-10 yrs (Revive), 10+ yrs (Bio-Boost / replacement opportunity)
  • Email 1 (Week 1) — "We have something new" — introduction to GoNano, link to landing page, no ask
  • Email 2 (Week 3) — "Loyalty pricing for past customers" — $X off per SQ as past-customer exclusive, valid 30 days
  • Email 3 (Week 5) — "Referral kicker" — refer a neighbor, both of you get $X off
  • SMS reminder through GoHighLevel automation at each step
  • Phone follow-up on anyone who opens 2+ emails

Investment & KPIs

  • Cost: ~$300 for copywriting + design. Email + SMS infrastructure already in place via GHL.
  • Primary KPI: 15+ inbound replies → 5+ booked inspections → 2-3 closes ($10-20K revenue)
  • Secondary KPI: Referral kicker generates 1-2 new neighbor leads
  • Multiplier: Past customers become repeat customers AND referral sources

Format ideas worth testing

  • Personalized roof age callout — "Your roof from 2022 is now Y years old, ideal Fortify window"
  • Loom-recorded message for top 20 past customers — 60-second personalized video

Play C · COMMUNITY Segment

Save A Roof Moncton Giveaway

June launch 8-12 week window 100-300 leads expected

The campaign's emotional centerpiece. A free GoNano rejuvenation given to one deserving Greater Moncton homeowner. Nominations come from the community, judging done by a Plus Ultra + NanoSeal panel. The story carries through local press, social, and a multi-month content runway.

Mechanics

  • Week 1-2 — Build landing page /save-a-roof with nomination form (email + phone + 500-word story)
  • Week 3 — Launch with press release to Times & Transcript, CBC NB, Acadie Nouvelle
  • Weeks 4-6 — Open nomination window. Paid Meta amplification + organic social + email blast.
  • Week 7 — Joint judging panel selects winner. Film reveal video.
  • Week 8 — Application day. Drone shots, before/after, family + crew photos.
  • Weeks 9-12 — Case study content, testimonial video, 6-10 social posts.

Investment & KPIs

  • Plus Ultra cash exposure: ~$1,200 + crew day
  • NanoSeal cash exposure: ~$700 + product (one home, ~$2K product cost)
  • Primary KPI: 100+ nominations submitted (=100+ leads in CRM)
  • Secondary KPI: 1+ piece of earned media coverage
  • Tertiary KPI: 10+ neighbour-of-winner door-knock conversations (= 2-3 close opportunities)

Optionality

  • Single winner (recommended — scarcity drives entries)
  • Two finalists with public vote (more engagement, more drama, slightly higher cost)

Play D · COLD Segment

Educational Livestream / Webinar Series

Monthly · June-Sept 30 min 200-500 registrations / ep.

A monthly 30-minute "Ask the Roofer" live session co-hosted by Mac and Ben. Educational, not sales-y. Frames Plus Ultra + NanoSeal as the rejuvenation authority in NB. Replays live on the blog + YouTube + Facebook for evergreen SEO value.

Format possibilities

  • Episode 1 (June) — "Your roof has more life left than you think" — intro to rejuvenation, when it works, when it doesn't
  • Episode 2 (July) — "Live roof scoring" — Mac walks the camera through a real roof using the GoNano rubric, asks audience to guess the score
  • Episode 3 (August) — "Behind the Save A Roof reveal" — interview the winner, document the before/after
  • Episode 4 (September) — "Winter prep + Q&A" — pre-winter checklist, open mic for audience questions

Channels & mechanics

  • Platform: StreamYard or Riverside → Facebook Live + YouTube Live + LinkedIn Live simultaneously
  • Promotion: Meta ads to "Moncton homeowners + interested in home improvement" 7 days out + day-of reminder
  • Registration: Lead capture via Plus Ultra landing page → routes to GHL
  • Replay distribution: Blog post + email to past customers + cut into 5-7 short clips for organic social

Investment & KPIs

  • Cost per episode: ~$200 ad spend + Mac/Ben time. StreamYard ~$30/mo.
  • Primary KPI: 200+ registrations per episode
  • Secondary KPI: 30+ live attendees, 1000+ replay views over 30 days
Alternative: One-off launch livestream If a monthly cadence is too much, do one hero livestream — "Greater Moncton's First Look at Roof Rejuvenation" — heavily promoted, 60-90 min, with all the energy of a launch event. Lower cadence pressure, higher single-event punch. Worth workshopping with Ben.

Reversibility check

Most of the campaign is reversible at low cost. The two hard commitments are: (1) the Save A Roof product donation (~$2K NanoSeal cost if we cancel mid-campaign) and (2) any vehicle wrap (~$1.5K sunk if we exit the partnership early). Everything else can be paused or pivoted within a week.
