The model

How the price is built.

Each tier prices off a divisor against hard cost. The divisor sets the gross margin. The richer the build, the higher the divisor headroom.

÷0.53

÷0.48

New deck. Hard cost is 48% of sell. 52% gross margin.

Why clay is cheaper

Ribbed vs European clay.

Same $230/sq panel, same trim and components. The only difference: ribbed rides on wood strapping, clay seats straight on the surface. Strapping runs about $45/sq, so on a 19 SQ roof that is roughly $855 of cost you drop on the clay build.

Where the margin goes

Gross margin to real profit.

Gross margin is not profit. Out of it comes overhead (20%), sales (10%) and marketing (5%) of the sale. What is left is net profit in your pocket.

The read

What this tells us.

  • Every package holds a healthy 47 to 52% gross margin by design. The divisor method is doing its job.
  • Premium standing seam is the profit engine at ~$9.4k net on Catherine, but its cost basis (SS ~$750/sq) is the softest number here. A real supply quote firms it.
  • Clay is not a discount on margin, it is a smaller bill of materials. You charge less and keep the same percentage.
  • Three inputs to lock: standing-seam material rate, strapping per square, and your real overhead / sales / marketing load. Confirm those and every number on these pages is exact.
