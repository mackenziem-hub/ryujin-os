03 / 10
Root cause

The tag never saw the booking

Bookings complete on a cross-origin calendar widget, not on the page where the conversion tag lives. So the tag never fired.

The one page that could have caught it, /success-booking, was an orphan. Nothing pointed visitors to it after they booked.

Where the break was

Calendar widget loads in a leadconnectorhq.com iframe. The Google tag sits on your own domain. Different origins, so the conversion event crossed a boundary the tag could not see.

The orphan page

/success-booking already had your Meta Pixel firing on it, but the calendar never redirected anyone there.

04 / 10
The fix

One unbroken path from click to recorded sale

Reuse the page that already existed. Point the calendar at it, make the page fire the conversion, and the ad click attribution rides all the way through on the first-party gclid cookie.

  1. Ad click lands with its gclid.
  2. Visitor books on the opt-in page.
  3. Calendar now redirects to /success-booking.
  4. That page fires the Google Ads conversion.
  5. Google records it against the original click.

Why we test on the real thing

Live checks caught two silent bugs

Both would have passed a code read and broken in production. Diffing the live calendar before and after each write is what surfaced them.

Bug 1 · the field names lie

GoHighLevel reads back one spelling of the redirect field and writes a different one. The first write looked correct and silently did nothing until we matched the write spelling.

Bug 2 · a hidden reset

The first successful write quietly knocked the inspection slot length from 60 minutes to 30, because the platform resets fields you do not resend. Caught in the before and after diff, restored to 60, and the tool now guards against it.

Net

Calendar is back to its exact original config, plus the redirect. Nothing else moved.
