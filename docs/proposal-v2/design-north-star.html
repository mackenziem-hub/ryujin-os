The recommendation

One premium, web-based experience. Not a PDF.

Across every reference, the winners share a spine and a feel. Here is the direction I would lock.

Open on the customer's own home and their inspection evidence, not a generic cover. Walk the proven persuasion spine. Present pricing as side-by-side good-better-best tiers the customer can actually click, with optional add-ons that toggle and re-total live. Close with one lock-in CTA and sign-on-the-spot. Borrow Roofr's roofing-evidence model, Qwilr's modular web-block format, and Proposify / Better Proposals' trust structure. Skin all of it in the Jewels system: milky cream canvas, royal-blue authority, a bronze premium accent, a serif display face over clean sans, and a lot of breathing room. Premium and calm, never busy.

Visual system

The palette and type

Cream is the canvas (Jewels rule), royal blue carries authority, bronze signals premium and marks the lock-in. Two faces only: a serif for headlines, Inter for body. Research had serif-heading + Inter-body at roughly +35% on conversion.

Your call

What I need from you to lock Phase 0

Pick these and I build the v2 stylesheet and section blocks straight to them. None of it is hard to change later, but settling them now keeps every proposal consistent.

  1. The vibe. Does the mockup above feel right (cream + royal blue + bronze, serif headlines, premium-calm)? Or pull it warmer, darker, more modern, more traditional?
  2. Web-interactive vs PDF-style. I am recommending the Qwilr-style interactive web page (selectable tiers, live totals, sign in-page). Confirm, or if you still need a clean PDF export, I build that as a render of the same page.
  3. The serif. Fraunces (shown) reads premium and a touch editorial. Playfair Display is more classic-luxury. I can drop in either to compare.
  4. Recommended tier. Mockup highlights Platinum as "Recommended." Keep Platinum as the default hero tier?
  5. Any reference to lean into harder. If one of the four feels most like "us," say so and I weight toward it.
